2011

The Founding Year

ILN was established as a non-profit Labuan foundation in Malaysia, governed by an international board.

Formed a key partnership with the Atlas Network, setting the stage for global outreach.

2014-15

Launching the Dialogue

2014

Hosted the 1st International Islam & Liberty Conference in Fez, Morocco, launching ILN's global dialogue on Islam and freedom.

2015

Organized the 2nd International Conference in Islamabad, Pakistan, expanding to South Asia.

Conducted the 1st Regional Residential Workshop in Qom, Iran, focusing on religious and economic freedom.

Published "Democratic Transitions in the Muslim World", a significant scholarly contribution.

2016-17

Expanding Horizons

2016

Held the 3rd International Conference in Istanbul, Turkey, emphasizing economic and political freedom.

Published "Islamic Foundations of a Free Society", exploring Islam's compatibility with free markets.

Co-published with the Institute of Economic Affairs, marking an important intellectual partnership.

2017

Hosted the 4th International Conference in Marrakesh, Morocco, strengthening North African engagement.

Conducted the 2nd Regional Residential Workshop in Tunis, Tunisia, promoting free enterprise locally.

2018-20

Achieving Milestones

2018

Organized the 5th International Conference in Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia, leveraging ILN's home base.

Published "Building an Islamic Case for Open Markets: History, Theory and Practice", focusing on economic theory.

2019

Held the 6th International Conference in Islamabad, Pakistan, reinforcing South Asian ties.

Published "An Islamic Case for Religious Freedom", advancing discussions on religious liberty.

2020

Hosted the 7th International Conference in Jakarta, Indonesia, reaching Southeast Asia.

Won the Asia Liberty Award from the Atlas Network for "Muslim Case for a Free Society", recognizing ILN's regional impact.

2021-22

Adapting to Change

2021

Adapted by hosting the 8th International Conference online, ensuring continuity during global challenges.

Conducted the 3rd Regional Residential Workshop in Muree, Pakistan, with scholars discussing freedom in Islam.

2022

Organized the 9th International Conference in Mardin, Turkey, focusing on regional Muslim world dynamics.

2023-24

Advancing the Mission

2023

Held the 10th International Conference in Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia, with 144 delegates and 20 international speakers.

2024

Hosted the 11th International Conference in Istanbul, Turkey, continuing ILN's global dialogue.

2025+

Future Directions

ILN aims to deepen its influence by enhancing its intellectual and outreach efforts, fostering dialogue on freedom in Muslim contexts.

Plans include expanding educational initiatives, strengthening global networks, and contributing to policy discussions, while keeping specific strategies confidential to maintain flexibility and impact.

Continued focus on empowering scholars, researchers, and communities to explore the compatibility of Islam with free societies.
